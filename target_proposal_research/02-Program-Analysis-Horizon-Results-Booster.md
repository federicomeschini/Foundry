# Program Analysis: Horizon Results Booster (HRB) Technical Manual

## 1. Mission and Consortium Ecosystem
The Horizon Results Booster (HRB) is the European Commission’s flagship initiative (DG RTD) to bridge the **"European Paradox"**—the gap between world-class research output and low market uptake.

### Consortium Partners & Roles:
*   **META Group (Italy):** Lead Contractor; provider of exploitation strategy and investment logic.
*   **PNO Consultants (Netherlands):** Specializes in large-scale market analysis and dissemination.
*   **EURICE (Germany):** Experts in IP management and European research management.
*   **ICONS (Italy):** Specializes in social impact analysis and science communication.
*   **Trust-IT (UK/Italy) & PPMI (Lithuania):** Manage the digital platform, data tracking, and EC policy reporting.

## 2. Technical Service Granularity

### Service 2: Business Plan Development (BPD)
*   **Objective:** Develop a production-ready business plan for **one Key Exploitable Result (KER)**.
*   **Deliverables:**
    1.  **KER Characterisation Table:** Technical definition of the innovation's TRL (Technology Readiness Level).
    2.  **Lean Canvas:** Rapid validation of the business model.
    3.  **The HRB Business Plan:** A structured document including TAM/SAM/SOM, revenue models, and **Time-to-Market** estimations.
    4.  **Financial Projection Sheet:** 3-5 year cash flow and CapEx requirements.
*   **Workflow:** 2-3 co-creative workshops (approx. 4 hours each) between the HRB Expert and the Project Consortium.

### Service 3: Go-to-Market Support (GTM)
*   **Objective:** Final-mile support for market entry and investor interaction.
*   **Sub-Modules & Technical Outputs:**
    *   **Module A (Exploitation Strategy):** Roadmap for licensing vs. spin-off.
    *   **Module B (Market Readiness):** Clinical assessment of the commercial landscape.
    *   **Module C (Investor Readiness):** Pitch deck critique and "Investor Ask" definition.
    *   **Module D (IP Management):** Guidance on patent protection and "Freedom to Operate" (FTO).
    *   **Module E (Funding Mapping):** Customized list of non-EU funding sources (VCs, Angels, EIC).

## 3. Operational Mechanics (The "Booster Journey")
The program is implemented through a centralized digital pipeline.

1.  **Application:** Projects apply via the **Booster Portal**, identifying their KERs.
2.  **Expert Matching:** The portal matches the KER's sector (e.g., Green Energy) with an expert's functional skillset (e.g., Financial Modeling in Energy).
3.  **Service Delivery Plan (SDP):** A legal and technical roadmap agreed upon during a preliminary call.
4.  **Final Report:** Prepared by the HRB Expert, summarizing recommendations and the matured deliverables.

## 4. Beneficiary Profile and Impact (2020-2028)
*   **Volume:** ~1,500 services delivered in Phase 1 (2020-2024); Phase 2 (2024-2028) has a **€14M+ budget**.
*   **Target Groups:**
    *   **Private Companies:** 40% of beneficiaries (SMEs and spin-offs).
    *   **Higher Education:** 25% (Universities/Labs).
    *   **Research Organizations:** 21%.
*   **Success Metric:** Number of **Exploitation Agreements** (licenses/spin-offs) signed within 12 months post-service.
