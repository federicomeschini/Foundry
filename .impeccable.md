## Design Context

### Users
Founders, startup operators, research commercialization teams, and program managers preparing a company or exploitable result for outside review. Used on desktop and laptop during business hours while refining a raise, a pilot pitch, or a commercialization package. Users are comfortable with dense information but want it structured cleanly.

### Brand Personality
OpenEconomics - **precise, institutional, considered**. The brand should read as a high-trust analytical advisor that helps make startup information legible without turning into a hype machine.

Fonts: Atkinson Hyperlegible Next (body/UI) + Hedvig Letters Serif (display emphasis)
Brand colors: Bluette (`oklch(34% 0.225 296)`) + Lime (`oklch(88% 0.20 129)`) as a sparse signal accent.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Analytical Editorial for Founders"** - a founder command room with the discipline of a research desk. Think coordinate grids, editorial spacing, controlled density, sharp labels, and a light theme built for daytime use. The product should feel more like a preparation instrument than a startup directory.

Anti-references: generic SaaS dashboards, startup marketplace grids, purple-gradient dark mode, consumer onboarding kits.

### Design Principles
1. **Lime is a signal, not decoration** - reserve it for readiness moments, focus, and high-value cues.
2. **Bluette is the brand anchor** - topbar, selected states, and key numbers; never use it as filler.
3. **Display type is for stakes, not volume** - hero numbers and the main product statement only.
4. **Preparation over spectacle** - the layout should help a founder inspect and tighten a company record, not perform startup coolness.
5. **Explainability must stay visible** - any score or status needs supporting context close by.
