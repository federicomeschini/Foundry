## Design Context

### Users
Sophisticated European investors and investment analysts evaluating early-stage startup opportunities. Used in offices and meeting rooms during business hours on desktop. The product is a screening and diligence workspace, not a consumer app — users are comfortable with dense information and expect precision.

### Brand Personality
OpenEconomics — **precise, institutional, considered**. A premier economic research and advisory firm. The brand is confident without being loud, rigorous without being cold.

Fonts: Atkinson Hyperlegible Next (body/UI) + Hedvig Letters Serif (display/numerics)
Brand colors: Bluette (`oklch(34% 0.225 296)` — deep navy indigo) + Lime (`oklch(88% 0.20 129)`) as a signal accent.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Analytical Editorial"** — Bloomberg Businessweek meets a premier European research desk. Think coordinate grids, massive serif numerals, tightly-set uppercase labels, and navy as an institutional anchor. Light theme (office daytime use). The product reads like a premium research publication, not a startup dashboard.

Anti-references: generic SaaS dashboards, AI-slop card grids, purple-gradient dark mode.

### Design Principles
1. **Lime is a signal, not decoration** — reserve for strong-fit indicators, topbar accent, and status pills only
2. **Bluette is the brand anchor** — topbar, primary actions, selected states, key metric values; never use it decoratively
3. **Numbers deserve display treatment** — hero ledger and dealroom scores use Hedvig Serif at large sizes
4. **Precision over softness** — 4px radius on controls, sharp gauge tracks (3px, no border-radius), tight grid (48px)
5. **Typography hierarchy must be legible at a glance** — uppercase 0.68rem labels, 1.25rem section heads, display type for hero
